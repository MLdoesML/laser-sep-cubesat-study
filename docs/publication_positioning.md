# Publication Positioning

## Core Claim

The publication should be framed as a mission-design and infrastructure co-design
paper for a CAPSTONE-class CubeSat architecture that combines onboard SEP with
laser power beaming from reusable cislunar nodes.

## What the Paper Should Claim

- a technically grounded co-design framework for trajectory and station
  placement
- a direct-capture case study for a CAPSTONE-class spacecraft
- an operations-aware evaluation of perigee boost and lunar braking
- a first look at infrastructure reuse for nearby mission variants

## What the Paper Should Not Claim

- that laser power beaming itself is novel
- that CubeSat low-thrust design is novel
- that cislunar logistics as a field is new
- that reusable cislunar infrastructure is solved in general

## Why the Study Can Still Be Novel

The likely novelty is the integrated combination of:

- CAPSTONE-class direct-capture geometry
- beam-augmented SEP rather than photon propulsion
- co-optimization of transfer orbit and station placement
- reusable infrastructure scoring
- truth-model validation with operational constraints

## Publication Risk

The project becomes weak if it turns into only one of the following:

- a generic low-thrust trajectory optimization paper
- a generic laser power-beaming concept note
- a speculative logistics paper without validated trajectory evidence

## Strong Result Package

The strongest result package contains:

- SEP-only baseline
- perigee-boost only comparison
- lunar-brake only comparison
- dual-window beam-assisted case
- explicit station placements
- uncertainty and outage sensitivity
- small reuse analysis across nearby missions

## Fallback Result Package

If direct capture does not fully close, the paper is still viable if it shows:

- where beam support helps
- where it fails to help
- which station placements are most attractive
- how infrastructure burden trades against mission benefit

## Likely Target Venues

- `Acta Astronautica`
- `Journal of Spacecraft and Rockets`
- `Journal of Guidance, Control, and Dynamics`
