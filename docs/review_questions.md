# Review Questions

Use this checklist before moving from study definition into heavy implementation.

## Mission Scope

- Is the CAPSTONE-class mass model the right anchor, or should the study move
  slightly lighter or slightly heavier?
- Is `GTO secondary` the right baseline launch lane for a first paper?
- Is the direct-capture success gate strict enough without becoming unrealistic?

## Architecture Scope

- Are two stations enough for the first paper?
- Are the chosen Earth-side and lunar-side station families the right minimum
  set?
- Is excluding ground lasers the right simplification for v1?

## Publication Scope

- Is the novelty claim still centered on co-design rather than on the beam
  technology itself?
- Is the logistics extension compact enough to support the paper without taking
  over the project?
- Does the study still read like one strong paper rather than two half-papers?

## Technical Scope

- Is the operational realism set large enough to be credible?
- Is the fixed-step search plus truth-model rerun the right balance for v1?
- Are the infrastructure and operations metrics specific enough to support a
  reusable-node argument?

## Go / No-Go Rule

Proceed to deeper implementation only if the answers above still support this
statement:

> This study can produce one technically defensible paper whose novelty is the
> co-design of a CAPSTONE-class direct-capture trajectory and reusable
> cislunar beam-station placement under realistic operational constraints.
